About this calculator

Most charitable tax credit calculators show you a number and call it a day. They don't check whether you actually owe enough tax to use the credit. This tool does.

Charitable donation tax credits in Canada are non-refundable — they reduce your tax owing, but can never push it below zero. If your income is low enough that you don't owe much tax, part or all of your credit may go unused. We think you should know that before you file.

How the calculation works

We estimate your federal and provincial income tax using your income and the basic personal amount. Then we calculate your charitable donation tax credit and compare it to your estimated tax. If the credit exceeds your tax, we show you exactly how much would go unused and what your options are.

What we assume

  • We estimate your tax using only your income and the basic personal amount — no other credits or deductions
  • We use the standard two-tier donation credit rates (first $200 / over $200) for all provinces — some provinces have additional tiers for very high earners that we don't model
  • We assume cash donations only — we don't handle donations of securities or ecologically sensitive land
  • We show an informational warning when your donation may exceed the CRA's 75% of net income annual claiming limit, but we cannot calculate the exact limit since we only know your gross income
  • Quebec is not supported — Quebec has a separate tax system administered by Revenu Québec

What this means for you

Our estimated tax will generally be higher than your actual tax, since most people have credits we don't account for (CPP/EI contributions, employment amount, age amount, and others). So when we warn that a credit may go unused because your tax is too low, the real situation could be even more limiting than what we show.
